Profile

"I am drawn to the questions that civilisations have forgotten to ask — and to the strange fact that they are always, at root, the same questions."

I'm a multidisciplinary academic scholar whose work refuses the usual borders between disciplines. Trained across Psychology, History, Egyptology, International Relations, and Policy Studies, I merely seek bring an unusual depth of cross-disciplinary synthesis to each inquiry.

A valedictorian graduate; I've pursued my intellectual formation with the conviction that the most urgent questions of the present — questions of identity, power, and governance, cannot be answered by any single background alone.

My work in Egyptology is characterised by an intrinsically driven inkling of ancient religious and political systems, and especially the expedition surrounding Cleopatra's tomb. My further engagement with International Relations draws on deep historical case studies that most IR scholarship ignores. After this, my writings in the Humanities is marked by a precision of language and a refusal of easy consolation, that comes from my civil services background.

And so, being disgustingly educated is the far-arching endeavor; one that sits at the core of the innate drive that pushes me towards the study of "eveything the sun" as they say.
